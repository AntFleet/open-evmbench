# Audit: 2024-07-benddao

## Isolate liquidation transfers the auctioned NFT to the caller instead of the winning bidder
- Location: src/libraries/logic/IsolateLogic.sol : executeIsolateLiquidate
- Mechanism: After an isolate auction ends, `executeIsolateLiquidate` consumes the winning bidder's escrowed funds (`loanData.bidAmount`, held in `assetData.totalBidAmout`) to repay the borrow (`erc20TransferOutBidAmountToLiqudity(debtAssetData, vars.totalBorrowAmount)`) and refunds any surplus to the borrower, but it then transfers the collateral NFT to `params.msgSender` (`erc721TransferOutLiquidity(nftAssetData, params.msgSender, ...)` / `erc721TransferIsolateSupplyOnLiquidate(..., params.msgSender, ...)`) rather than to `loanData.lastBidder`. `validateIsolateLiquidateLoan`/`validateIsolateLiquidateBasic` impose no check that the caller is the winning bidder, so any address can call it once `block.timestamp > bidStartTimestamp + auctionDuration`. Because a valid bid was required to be `>= borrowAmount`, the only out-of-pocket cost to the caller is `vars.totalExtraAmount = max(0, borrowAmount - bidAmount)`, i.e. just the interest accrued during the auction window.
- Impact: Any third party (or the defaulting borrower themselves) can call `isolateLiquidate` on a finished auction to seize the NFT for ~free while the legitimate winning bidder's entire escrowed bid is consumed and they receive nothing, directly stealing the collateral.

## Yield-staking unstake/repay operate on the caller's yield account instead of the position's
- Location: src/yield/YieldStakingBase.sol : _unstake / _repay (and src/yield/lido/YieldEthStakingLido.sol : protocolRequestWithdrawal/protocolClaimWithdraw)
- Mechanism: `_unstake` and `_repay` permit `msg.sender == botAdmin` to act on another user's NFT (`require(vars.nftOwner == msg.sender || botAdmin == msg.sender, ...)`), but they resolve the yield account with `yieldAccounts[msg.sender]` and the protocol withdrawal/claim helpers also read `yieldAccounts[msg.sender]`. The position's real account is stored in `sd.yieldAccount` (set during `_stake`, and correctly used by `_getNftYieldInUnderlyingAsset`). When the bot liquidates a user's position, `convertToYieldAssets`, `accountYieldShares`, `accountYieldInWithdraws`, and the Lido/EtherFi `requestWithdrawals`/`claimWithdraw` calls are all applied to the bot's own account rather than the staker's, since `sd.yieldShare` (the staker's shares) is decremented from the bot's `accountYieldShares`.
- Impact: Bot-driven liquidation of unhealthy leveraged staking positions either reverts (share underflow / bot has no yield account) or withdraws and burns the bot's own staked funds to repay someone else's debt, so undercollateralized positions cannot be liquidated and the protocol accrues bad debt.

## Isolate redeem (and repay) reduce the wrong account's scaled debt with no borrower check
- Location: src/libraries/logic/IsolateLogic.sol : executeIsolateRedeem (and executeIsolateRepay)
- Mechanism: `executeIsolateRedeem` decrements isolate debt with `VaultLogic.erc20DecreaseIsolateScaledBorrow(debtGroupData, params.msgSender, vars.amountScaled)` while simultaneously decrementing `loanData.scaledAmount` of the loan being redeemed, yet neither `validateIsolateRedeemBasic` nor `validateIsolateRedeemLoan` verifies that `msg.sender` is the loan's borrower (`tokenData.owner`) or an approved operator, and there is no `onBehalf` parameter. Any address that itself holds isolate debt in the same group can therefore redeem another user's auctioned loan: the loan is reset to `LOAN_STATUS_ACTIVE` and its `scaledAmount` is reduced, the last bidder is refunded, but the borrower's `userScaledIsolateBorrow` is never touched (the caller's is reduced instead), desynchronizing per-user isolate accounting from `loanData.scaledAmount` and `group.totalScaledIsolateBorrow`. `executeIsolateRepay` has the same root cause, decrementing `params.onBehalf` without confirming it owns the targeted loan.
- Impact: A holder of isolate debt can repeatedly cancel other users' auctions (perpetually shielding underwater loans from liquidation) and corrupt the protocol's per-user isolate borrow bookkeeping, leaving borrowers with phantom debt and the group totals inconsistent.

## Chainlink price feed staleness is not validated
- Location: src/PriceOracle.sol : getAssetPriceFromChainlink
- Mechanism: The function reads `latestRoundData()` and only enforces `answer > 0`, `updatedAt != 0`, and `answeredInRound >= roundId`. It never compares `updatedAt` against `block.timestamp` with a per-feed heartbeat/staleness window, and `answeredInRound`/`roundId` are returned from the same call so that comparison is effectively a no-op. Consequently a frozen, halted, or deprecated feed continues to return its last value indefinitely, and this price flows into every collateral/debt valuation via `getAssetPrice` (health factor, LTV, liquidation, and NFT-base-currency conversion through `getAssetPriceFromBendNFTOracle`).
- Impact: If a Chainlink feed stalls, an attacker can borrow against stale-inflated collateral or avoid/force liquidations at an outdated price, leaving the protocol with bad debt.
