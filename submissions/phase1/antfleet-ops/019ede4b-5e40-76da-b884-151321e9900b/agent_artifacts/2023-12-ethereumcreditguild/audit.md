# Audit: 2023-12-ethereumcreditguild

## Permanent Locking of CREDIT in SurplusGuildMinter due to Blanket applyGaugeLoss
- Location: `src/loan/SurplusGuildMinter.sol` : `unstake` / `getRewards` & `src/tokens/GuildToken.sol` : `applyGaugeLoss`
- Mechanism: The `SurplusGuildMinter` contract holds GUILD tokens on behalf of all users who stake CREDIT through it, acting as a single entity in the `GuildToken` gauge system. When a loss occurs in a gauge, `GuildToken.notifyGaugeLoss` is called. Any external actor can then permissionlessly call `GuildToken.applyGaugeLoss(gauge, SurplusGuildMinter)`, which burns **all** GUILD tokens held by the `SurplusGuildMinter` contract for that gauge and zeroes out its weight. However, `SurplusGuildMinter.getRewards` only slashes individual users if `lastGaugeLoss > userStake.lastGaugeLoss`. If a user stakes *after* the loss is notified but *before* `applyGaugeLoss` is called, their `userStake.lastGaugeLoss` is initialized to the current `lastGaugeLoss`. When `applyGaugeLoss` is subsequently called, it burns the new user's GUILD tokens along with everyone else's. When this new user later attempts to `unstake`, `getRewards` will not mark them as slashed (since their `lastGaugeLoss` matches the gauge's), and the `unstake` function will attempt to call `GuildToken.decrementGauge`. This call will revert due to an underflow, because the `SurplusGuildMinter`'s gauge weight was already entirely zeroed out by the blanket `applyGaugeLoss` call.
- Impact: Users who stake through the `SurplusGuildMinter` after a loss notification but before the loss application will have their GUILD tokens burned and their CREDIT permanently locked in the surplus buffer, as they can never successfully call `unstake`. An attacker could intentionally monitor for new stakes and immediately call `applyGaugeLoss` to grief users, or this could occur naturally through routine protocol operations.
