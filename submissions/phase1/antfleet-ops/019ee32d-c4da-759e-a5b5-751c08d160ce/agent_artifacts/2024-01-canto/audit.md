# Audit: 2024-01-canto

## System Broken due to Block Number and Timestamp Unit Mismatch in Reward Calculation
- Location: `LendingLedger.sol` : `update_market`
- Mechanism: In `LendingLedger.sol`, `update_market` calculates the weekly epoch as `epoch = (i / BLOCK_EPOCH) * BLOCK_EPOCH;` based on the block number variable `i` (where `BLOCK_EPOCH = 100_000` blocks). It then queries the relative weight of the gauge by calling `gaugeController.gauge_relative_weight_write(_market, epoch)`. However, `GaugeController.sol` is based on Curve's Vyper template, which runs on time-based units and expects the parameter `_time` to be a Unix timestamp in seconds (e.g., ~1.7 billion). Passing a block number (like 100,000 or 200,000) causes the gauge controller to look up weights in the year 1970. Because no votes or checkpoints existed back then, it returns `0`.
- Impact: Since `gauge_relative_weight_write` always returns 0, the reward per block `cantoReward` is always evaluated as 0. As a result, no CANTO rewards are ever earned or distributed to any lending market, completely bricking the protocol's core functionality.

## Accounting Error in Epoch Interval Calculation of `update_market`
- Location: `LendingLedger.sol` : `update_market`
- Mechanism: Inside `update_market()`, the loop that attempts to distribute rewards across epochs calculates the end boundary of the loop's current iteration as `uint256 nextEpoch = i + BLOCK_EPOCH;`. This is functionally incorrect because `nextEpoch` should be the start of the next chronological epoch, which is `epoch + BLOCK_EPOCH` (where `epoch = (i / BLOCK_EPOCH) * BLOCK_EPOCH`). Because the ledger updates `lastRewardBlock` to `block.number` every time a user deposits or withdraws (via `sync_ledger`), `i` will always start at highly variable, non-aligned block numbers. Since users transact frequently, the window `block.number - i` is almost always less than `BLOCK_EPOCH` (100k blocks), meaning `Math.min(nextEpoch, block.number)` will always resolve to `block.number`. Consequently, the loop executes only once, treating the entire interval as a single block delta under the initial block's epoch rate, even if that interval crossed an actual epoch boundary.
- Impact: The contract fails to transition and update reward rates across epoch boundaries. This leads to massive accounting errors and discrepancies in rewards, which users can easily exploit by selectively timing their withdrawals or deposits to extend high-reward epochs or skip low-reward epochs.

## Corruption of Global Slope and Bias on Vote Cancellation After Gauge Removal
- Location: `GaugeController.sol` : `vote_for_gauge_weights` (interaction with `_remove_gauge_weight`)
- Mechanism: When governance removes a gauge using `remove_gauge`, it invokes `_remove_gauge_weight`, which zeroes out all the upcoming slope changes for that gauge in `changes_weight` and decrements those slope values from the global `changes_sum` to clean up the state. However, if a user who previously voted on that gauge later modifies their vote to `0` (which they must do to recover their voting power), the `vote_for_gauge_weights` function executes. It attempts to cancel the user's old scheduled slope changes again by subtracting `old_slope.slope` from `changes_sum[old_slope.end]`. Since this subtraction was already performed when the gauge was removed, it underflows the actual remaining slope contributions from other active gauges, capping `changes_sum` at 0 or decrementing it incorrectly.
- Impact: The scheduled global slope decreases for valid, active gauges are wiped out or reduced. This causes the total weight of the system (`points_sum`) to retain an artificially inflated bias forever, deflating the relative weights of all legitimate gauges and permanently underproducing rewards for all users.

## Permanent Reward Lockup on Lending Market Removal from Whitelist
- Location: `LendingLedger.sol` : `claim` (and `update_market` modifier checks)
- Mechanism: When a user calls `claim()` to withdraw their accrued CANTO rewards for a specific market, the contract invokes `update_market(_market)`. The first line of `update_market()` asserts that the market is whitelisted via `require(lendingMarketWhitelist[_market], "Market not whitelisted")`. If governance unwhitelists (removes) a compromised or deprecated lending market by setting `lendingMarketWhitelist[_market] = false`, this requirement will revert.
- Impact: Users who have already earned rewards in a deprecated/removed market will be permanently blocked from calling `claim()` or `sync_ledger()`, completely freezing and locking up their accumulated CANTO rewards forever.

## Denial of Service (DoS) in `update_market` via Checked Safe-Casting Overflow
- Location: `LendingLedger.sol` : `update_market`
- Mechanism: In `LendingLedger.sol`, `accCantoPerShare` and `secRewardsPerShare` are declared as `uint128`. During reward distribution inside `update_market`, the reward increment `(cantoReward * 1e18) / marketSupply` is explicitly cast to `uint128`. If a lending market has an extremely low supply (e.g. 1 wei of cNOTE remaining or during early initialization), the division `(cantoReward * 1e18) / marketSupply` can yield an extremely high amount that exceeds `type(uint128).max` ($3.4 \times 10^{38}$). Under Solidity 0.8+, safe casting is strictly checked and will automatically revert upon overflow.
- Impact: The entire reward mechanism for the affected market will be permanently DOSed and bricked. Because `update_market` is also called inside `sync_ledger` during any deposit or withdrawal, users will become unable to withdraw their deposits or interact with the lending ledger at all.
