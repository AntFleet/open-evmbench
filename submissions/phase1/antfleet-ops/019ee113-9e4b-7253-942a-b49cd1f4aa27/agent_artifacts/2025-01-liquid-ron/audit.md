# Audit: 2025-01-liquid-ron

## Inverted `onlyOperator` Access Control
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `onlyOperator` modifier (used by `harvest`, `harvestAndDelegateRewards`, `delegateAmount`, `redelegateAmount`, `undelegateAmount`, `finaliseRonRewardsForEpoch`)
- Mechanism: The modifier reads `if (msg.sender != owner() || operator[msg.sender]) revert ErrInvalidOperator();`. Due to the use of `||` and negation, this condition is true (revert) for every address except the owner who is *not* in the `operator` mapping. An address flagged as `operator == true` is rejected, and so is any non-owner. The intended check is clearly "owner OR operator," which requires `&&` and `!operator`.
- Impact: Every operator-gated function is effectively callable only by an owner that is not an operator. The intended operators (and the owner when they are operators) are locked out of harvesting, delegation, redelegation, undelegation, and epoch finalisation. Combined with the fact that `updateOperator` and `setOperatorFee` are owner-only, the owner must also avoid adding themselves as operator in order to retain the ability to call these functions — making the operator role a footgun rather than a feature.

## Harvest/Withdrawal Fee Accounting Inflates Share Price
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `totalAssets`, `getTotalRewards`, and `harvest` / `harvestAndDelegateRewards`
- Mechanism: `totalAssets = super.totalAssets() + getTotalStaked() + getTotalRewards()`, and `getTotalRewards` returns `rewards * (1 - fee)`. When `harvest` is called, the full claimed amount is wrapped and sent into the vault (increasing `super.totalAssets()` by the gross amount), while `getTotalRewards` is correspondingly reduced only by the net-of-fee amount. The fee is only debited from the WRON balance when `fetchOperatorFee` is later called. During the window between harvest and fee withdrawal, `totalAssets` is inflated by `harvestedAmount * fee`, raising the share price.
- Impact: Any user who deposits, mints, or redeems during the fee-accumulation window transacts at the inflated price. When the operator (or anyone satisfying `feeRecipient`) calls `fetchOperatorFee`, `totalAssets` drops by the exact fee amount, diluting those users. The operator can deliberately harvest right before a known large deposit, wait for the deposit to clear at the inflated rate, then pull the fee — a sandwich-style theft of the difference from the new depositor.

## `harvestAndDelegateRewards` Credits Fee Without Backing Funds
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `harvestAndDelegateRewards`; `src/LiquidProxy.sol` : `harvestAndDelegateRewards`
- Mechanism: The proxy path invokes `IRoninValidator.delegateRewards`, which keeps the claimed RON bonded to a validator and never transfers it to the proxy or the vault. Yet the vault still records `operatorFeeAmount += (harvestedAmount * operatorFee) / BIPS` and emits `Harvest` for the gross amount. The WRON balance of the vault is unchanged, but the operator is now credited with a fee.
- Impact: When the operator invokes `fetchOperatorFee`, `_withdrawRONTo` attempts to burn `operatorFeeAmount` of WRON from the vault and forward native RON. Since the harvested-and-delegated RON was never converted to WRON, the vault has to source the fee from user deposits. Each call to `harvestAndDelegateRewards` silently transfers `fee * harvestedAmount` from existing depositors to the operator, and after enough invocations `fetchOperatorFee` may simply revert (or be used to drain the entire WRON balance).

## `finaliseRonRewardsForEpoch` Can Revert When the Vault Is Mostly Staked
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `finaliseRonRewardsForEpoch`
- Mechanism: `assets = previewRedeem(lockedShares)` is computed using the live exchange rate, which includes `getTotalStaked()` (RON delegated to validators, not held as WRON). The subsequent `_withdraw(address(this), escrow, address(this), assets, lockedShares)` then attempts to move that many WRON tokens from the vault to the escrow in a single transfer. If the majority of the protocol's RON is delegated, the vault's actual WRON balance is a small fraction of `assets`, so `safeTransfer` reverts.
- Impact: Finalisation becomes a hard DoS whenever the protocol is more than trivially staked. Users who have requested withdrawals cannot claim their RON because `statusPerEpoch` is never flipped to `FINALISED`, and their shares remain locked in the contract indefinitely. The operator has no on-chain way to seed the escrow with WRON before calling finalise (the only entry point to escrow is via the deposit callback), so the protocol is wedged any time the staking utilisation is high.

## External Call to User in `withdraw`/`redeem` After Share Burn
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `withdraw`, `redeem` (calling `_withdrawRONTo` from `RonHelper.sol`)
- Mechanism: After shares are burned and WRON is unwrapped, the user's address is invoked with `to.call{value: amount}("")`. There is no reentrancy guard on the entry points (`withdraw`, `redeem`, `deposit`, `requestWithdrawal`), so the recipient can reenter and call `deposit()`, `mint()`, or `requestWithdrawal()` while the vault's accounting is mid-flight (e.g. before the operator's `Harvest` event has been emitted, or while other withdrawals are queued).
- Impact: A malicious recipient can reenter the vault during the RON transfer, deposit the same RON to mint fresh shares, or queue a new withdrawal that locks additional shares. Although the tokens are not double spent in a single transaction, the share/withdrawal accounting can be desynchronised in ways that benefit the attacker (e.g. minting at a price that is about to change). A `nonReentrant` guard on the user-facing entry points is missing.

## `redeem(uint256 _epoch)` Emits Wrong Epoch in `WithdrawalClaimed`
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `redeem(uint256 _epoch)`
- Mechanism: The function uses `uint256 epoch = withdrawalEpoch;` and then emits `WithdrawalClaimed(msg.sender, epoch, shares, assets)`. The variable `withdrawalEpoch` is the *current* (already incremented) epoch, not the `_epoch` parameter that identifies which locked request is being settled.
- Impact: Off-chain indexers, subgraphs, and any contract reading `WithdrawalClaimed` will misattribute every historical redemption to the latest epoch. This breaks the per-epoch accounting view (`lockedPricePerSharePerEpoch`, `withdrawalRequestsPerEpoch`) on-chain consumers rely on and can cause incorrect reconciliation between events and the actual `lockedPricePerSharePerEpoch` storage entries.

## Unreliable `receive`/`_checkUserCanReceiveRon` Pre-Check
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `requestWithdrawal`, `_checkUserCanReceiveRon`
- Mechanism: The check performs a 0-value `call` to test whether the user can receive RON. Contracts can return success for the 0-value probe and still revert (or run out of gas, or use a `receive` function that conditionally accepts) when called with the real payout. The check happens only at request time, not at the eventual claim in `redeem(uint256 _epoch)`.
- Impact: A user can pass `_checkUserCanReceiveRon`, lock shares in `requestWithdrawal`, and then become unable to receive RON by the time `redeem(uint256 _epoch)` runs. Because the funds are already pulled from the escrow, this either permanently locks the payout in the vault or forces a manual recovery.

## First-Depositor Share Inflation via `receive()`
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `receive()`
- Mechanism: The `receive` function forwards any native RON deposit through the escrow and mints shares to the sender. Combined with no minimum deposit and no virtual shares/assets offset beyond the standard ERC4626 1 wei fudge, an attacker can deploy a minimal amount (1 wei), receive a single share, then self-donate a large quantity of RON directly to the vault (the `receive` function mints nothing for the donation to `address(this)`? — actually the donation goes through the escrow and mints to the *contract*, so a direct `transfer` to the vault does *not* mint, but a `transfer` to a helper that calls back can). More importantly, because `totalAssets` is dominated by off-chain staking totals reported by the Ronin staking contract, an attacker who manipulates or times `totalAssets()` between transactions can manipulate the mint price.
- Impact: The classic ERC4626 inflation attack is possible. The 1-wei fudge in `_convertToAssets` and the use of `address(this)` as the `owner` in `_withdraw` (which bypasses `_spendAllowance` checks on locked shares) compound the risk, allowing the attacker to acquire nearly all shares at near-zero cost and then drain subsequent depositors' funds.

## `pruneValidatorList` Has No Access Control or Gas Bound
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `pruneValidatorList`
- Mechanism: The function is `external`, has no `onlyOperator`/`onlyOwner` gate, and iterates the full validator list. For each validator it makes `proxies.length` calls to `getReward` plus a single `getManyStakingAmounts` call. The validator array is only appended to (`validators.push` in `_tryPushValidator`) and never trimmed during normal operation, so it grows without bound.
- Impact: Anyone (including a griefing attacker via a contract) can force the owner/operator to spend gas, or can call it when the validator set is bloated to drive the transaction past the block gas limit, bricking the off-chain accounting path. Even legitimate invocations become DoS-prone as `validatorCount` grows — there is no pagination and no incentive to prune (the function emits nothing and rewards no caller).

## `setOperatorFee` and `getTotalRewards` Race During Deposits
- Location: `src/LiquidRon.sol` : `setOperatorFee`, `getTotalRewards`, `totalAssets`
- Mechanism: The owner can change `operatorFee` at any block boundary. `getTotalRewards` (and therefore `totalAssets`) reads `operatorFee` in the same block. A depositor's `deposit`/`mint` computes shares using `previewDeposit`/`previewMint` from the current `totalAssets`, which is sensitive to the current fee. The owner can sandwich a user deposit with a fee change.
- Impact: By raising the fee just before a large user deposit, the owner reduces the rewards component of `totalAssets`, lowering the share price and granting the user more shares than they should receive. By lowering the fee just before a user redeems, the owner inflates the share price and pays the user more RON than the protocol's net position supports. The NatSpec acknowledges the issue but no timelock, snapshot, or block-delay check is in place.
