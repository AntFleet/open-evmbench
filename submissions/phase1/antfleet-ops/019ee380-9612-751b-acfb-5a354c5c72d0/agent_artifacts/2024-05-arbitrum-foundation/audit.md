# Audit: 2024-05-arbitrum-foundation

I audited the contracts (this is the Arbitrum Nitro/BOLD stack: assertion/edge staking pools, bridge/inbox/outbox, sequencer inbox + delay buffer, and the edge challenge manager). The bulk of this is the hardened, previously-audited Arbitrum code and I did not find an exploitable break in the bridge, outbox spend-bitmap, sequencer accumulator, delay-buffer math, or the edge bisection/confirmation logic. The one genuine issue I can stand behind is in the staking-pool accounting layer.

## Loss socialization via early pooled-stake withdrawal in challenge staking pools
- Location: `src/assertionStakingPool/AbsBoldStakingPool.sol` : `withdrawFromPool(uint256)` (and the no-arg overload), interacting with `AssertionStakingPool.createAssertion` / `EdgeStakingPool.createEdge`
- Mechanism: The pool tracks each depositor's contribution in `depositBalance[msg.sender]` but commingles all tokens in a single balance. `withdrawFromPool` only checks `amount <= depositBalance[msg.sender]` and pays out of the pool's current token balance on a first-come-first-served basis; there is no link between the pooled funds and whether the stake that was forwarded into the rollup/challenge manager is still recoverable. The contract header asserts the design is safe because "the protocol move will still be created once the required stake amount is reached, and all depositors will still be eventually refunded." That assumption silently ignores the slashing path: a mini-stake placed on a *losing* assertion/edge is not returned. When `createAssertion`/`createEdge` forwards `requiredStake` into the protocol and that assertion/edge is later rejected, those tokens are gone, but each depositor's `depositBalance` is still recorded at its full original value. Because withdrawals are unconditional and paid FIFO from whatever tokens remain, depositors who withdraw first (e.g. monitoring mempool and racing the loss, or who pulled "excess" before the move resolves) recover their full balance, while depositors who withdraw last absorb the entire shortfall.
- Impact: In any case where the pool-created assertion or edge is the losing side of a dispute (honestly-mistaken validators, or an attacker who intentionally seeds a pool, triggers the protocol move, then front-runs withdrawals), the realized slash is not shared pro-rata across depositors. Early withdrawers are made whole at the direct expense of co-depositors, who can lose up to their entire stake even though they each contributed an equal fraction of the slashed amount. This is an accounting/fairness flaw enabling one depositor to socialize their loss onto others rather than the per-depositor isolation the `depositBalance` mapping implies.

Notes on things I deliberately did **not** flag as vulnerabilities:
- "Tokens sent directly to the pool are lost" and the assumption that the rollup never returns more than was deposited are explicitly documented invariants, not bugs.
- The pools assume a well-behaved (non-fee-on-transfer, non-rebasing) stake token; under a misbehaving token the `depositBalance += amount` before `safeTransferFrom` accounting would drift, but that is the same documented token-restriction assumption used throughout the bridge.
