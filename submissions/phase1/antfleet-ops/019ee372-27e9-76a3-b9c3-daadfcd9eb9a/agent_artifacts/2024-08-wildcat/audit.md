# Audit: 2024-08-wildcat

## Impossible to Close Market Due to Strict Debt Refund and Dual Truncation
- Location: `src/market/WildcatMarket.sol` : `closeMarket`
- Mechanism: `closeMarket()` forcefully withdraws any pre-existing excess asset balances back to the borrower, guaranteeing that `currentlyHeld` equals exactly `totalDebts`. `availableLiquidity` is therefore strictly set to `state.normalizeAmount(state.scaledTotalSupply)`. When the function attempts to pay out pending or unpaid withdrawal batches, it limits the payout to `scaledAvailableLiquidity`, calculated using `state.scaleAmount(availableLiquidity)`. Because both `normalizeAmount` and `scaleAmount` use integer division that rounds down, the calculation `scaleAmount(normalizeAmount(scaledTotalSupply))` can evaluate to slightly less than `scaledTotalSupply` (e.g. `3 * 1.5e27 / 1e27 = 4`; `4 * 1e27 / 1.5e27 = 2 < 3`). This produces a phantom shortfall where a tiny fraction of a scaled withdrawal remains "unpaid," causing the batch to stay open and the function to reliably revert with `CloseMarketWithUnpaidWithdrawals()`.
- Impact: A borrower can permanently lose the ability to close their market. Donating extra underlying asset tokens to the contract to bypass the shortfall fails since `closeMarket()` aggressively refunds extra collateral before processing withdrawals, rendering the 1-2 wei discrepancy unsolvable and trapping the market in an open state where interest continues to accrue forever.
